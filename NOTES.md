# NOTES.md

Best configuration: BPE tokenizer (256 merges, vocab 512, trained only on train_corpus.txt, verified lossless) replacing the byte-level baseline, since Devanagari text was costing 3 bytes/token under the raw byte-level scheme. Architecture: n_embd 152, n_layer 6, block_size 256, weight tying, scaled residual init (std scaled by (2*n_layer)^-0.5 on projection/MLP-out layers) — 1,792,384 params, under the 2,000,000 cap. Optimizer: AdamW (weight decay 0.1, excluded from 1-D params), gradient clipping at 1.0, 100-step linear warmup into cosine decay to 10% of peak LR, peak LR 1e-3, batch size 16, 2000 steps (at the step cap). This configuration scored dev bpb 2.073, a 12.6% reduction from the baseline's 2.3718.

An ablation (Run 3) isolating the tokenizer alone, holding the baseline architecture and optimizer fixed, showed the BPE tokenizer captured nearly the entire early gain (2.3718 → 2.2826) seen when the tokenizer, architecture, and optimizer were changed together (Run 2: 2.2833) — meaning the architecture/optimizer changes contributed almost nothing on their own until LR was retuned. Raising peak LR (Runs 4–5, 6e-4 → 8e-4 → 1e-3) let the larger model exploit its added capacity within the fixed step budget, closing that gap and then improving further. The final large jump (Run 5 → Run 6, 2.272 → 2.073) came from increasing batch size to 16, which likely reduced gradient noise enough for the already-tuned LR schedule to converge more effectively — though this was not isolated from a possible batch×LR interaction, and the run's wall-clock time was disproportionately higher than expected, so we can't rule out machine load as a partial confound. Given the fixed 120-minute budget, we prioritized the highest-ROI ablation (tokenizer) and accepted a combined, non-orthogonal final change (batch size) rather than fully isolating every factor.
